Notification Accessibility - How it Works

Notification Carbon Components are messages that provide information to a user. The WAI-ARIA role="alert" is used to display a message to the user in a way that attracts the user's attention without interrupting their current task. Therefore, a notification should not affect the keyboard focus. The close button contains an ARIA label, aria-label="close", and the icon has aria-hidden="true" so it is ignored by assistive technologies. The Tab key is used to move focus to the close button within the notification and the Space key or Enter key activate the button to close the notification.

Accessibility Considerations

  • Avoid designing critical or important alerts that disappear automatically, such as a Toast Notification. An alert that disappears too quickly can lead to failure to meet WCAG 2.0 success criterion 2.2.3.
  • Avoid frequent interruptions that inhibit usability for people with visual and cognitive disabilities, which also make meeting the requirements of WCAG 2.0 success criterion 2.2.4 more difficult.
  • Ensure the use of color and hidden icons are not used as the only means of conveying the importance of the notification.
